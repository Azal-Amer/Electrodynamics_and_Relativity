Relativistic Particle Motion in Electromagnetic Fields

A charged particle experiencing the relativistic Lorentz force \( \frac{d\vec{p}}{dt} = q(\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \). Keep in mind that everything here is defined with
respect to the lab (stationary) frame, and the only difference from the nonrelativistic regime is that we use relativistic momentum \( \vec{p}=\gamma(v)m\vec{v} \).

\(\gamma = 1\)

Electric Field (E)

Ex: 0
Ey: 0
Ez: 1

Magnetic Field (B)

Bx: 0
By: 1
Bz: 0

Initial Velocity (v)

vx: 0.1
vy: 0
vz: 0
